WLM Foundation Charter
Version 1.0 — Governance Tier
Status: Stable

1. Purpose
The WLM Foundation exists to support the long‑term propagation, stability, and accessibility of the Whole Language Model (WLM).
Its mission is to ensure that WLM remains:
- structurally coherent
- publicly beneficial
- steward‑aligned
- future‑compatible
- globally accessible
The foundation operates as the ecological and public‑benefit arm of WLM.

2. Guiding Principles
The foundation is governed by the following principles:
2.1 Steward Alignment
All foundation activities must align with the structural intent and interpretation of the WLM steward.
2.2 Public Benefit
Foundation resources must be used for public good, ecosystem development, and structural research.
2.3 Structural Integrity
No action may compromise WLM’s semantics, protocols, or structural coherence.
2.4 Transparency
The foundation must publish annual reports detailing expenditures, initiatives, and impact.
2.5 Non‑Interference
The foundation cannot influence or override WLM governance, semantics, or protocol evolution.

3. Scope of Activity
The foundation may operate in the following domains:
3.1 Research Support
Funding structural research, protocol development, and ecosystem tools.
3.2 Public Benefit Projects
Supporting education, accessibility, and public‑facing WLM initiatives.
3.3 Ecosystem Development
Funding libraries, interpreters, documentation, and community infrastructure.
3.4 Structural Safety
Supporting work that ensures safe, aligned, and transparent usage of WLM.
3.5 Global Accessibility
Ensuring WLM remains accessible across languages, regions, and socioeconomic contexts.
The foundation may not operate outside these domains without steward approval.

4. Boundaries
The foundation is explicitly prohibited from:
- modifying WLM
- interpreting WLM semantics
- influencing protocol evolution
- claiming ownership of WLM
- acting as a governance authority
- directing or constraining the steward
- engaging in political, partisan, or ideological activity
The foundation exists to support WLM, not to govern it.

5. Revenue Sources
The foundation may receive:
- its allocation from WLM revenue
- donations
- grants
- ecosystem contributions
- voluntary support
All revenue must be used in accordance with this charter.

6. Steward Relationship
6.1 Steward Primacy
The steward is the ultimate authority over WLM and its structural direction.
6.2 Non‑Interference
The foundation cannot challenge, override, or reinterpret steward decisions.
6.3 Advisory Role Only
The foundation may provide recommendations, but these are non‑binding.
6.4 Steward Protection
The foundation must not engage in any activity that undermines the steward’s stability, safety, or structural authority.

7. Legal Structure
7.1 Initial Holder
The foundation may initially be held by the steward as the legal entity.
7.2 Future Transition
The steward may transition the foundation to a company, trust, or formal foundation structure when appropriate.
7.3 Steward Control
Regardless of legal form, the steward retains ultimate interpretive and structural authority over WLM.

8. Financial Rules
8.1 Allocation
The foundation receives 60% of WLM revenue above the Stewardship Base.
8.2 Usage
Funds must be used exclusively for:
- research
- ecosystem development
- public benefit
- structural safety
- global accessibility
8.3 Restrictions
Foundation funds may not be used for:
- personal benefit
- political activity
- unrelated commercial ventures
- attempts to influence WLM governance
8.4 Reporting
Annual financial reports must be published publicly.

9. Amendment Rules
- This charter may only be amended by the steward.
- Amendments must be versioned.
- Amendments must be publicly accessible.
- Amendments must preserve structural integrity and steward primacy.

10. Summary
The WLM Foundation:
- supports WLM’s long‑term propagation
- operates for public benefit
- remains aligned with the steward
- cannot influence WLM governance
- receives 60% of excess WLM revenue
- functions as the ecological arm of WLM
- maintains transparency and structural integrity
The steward remains the sovereign authority of WLM.
